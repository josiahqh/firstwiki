# The Circle She Carries

She arrives in a rush of light and crying,
curled like a question mark against your chest —
this small, astonishing fact of a person,
still carrying the scent of somewhere else.

Watch her learn the grammar of her legs,
each stumble a rough draft, each rise a revision.
She names the dog, the moon, the color blue
three times before she trusts that things persist.

Then she is running. Always running.
Hair a flag behind her, laughter trailing
like ribbon unspooled from some inexhaustible spool
inside a chest that doesn't know it's finite.

The years do what years do — insist.
She grows into opinions, distances, a self
that needs a room with a door that shuts.
You learn to knock. She learns to sometimes open.

There is a morning she looks in the mirror
and sees, flickering beneath her own face,
the ghost of yours. She doesn't say so.
You don't say you've always been there, watching.

She loves someone. She loses sleep.
She plants things. She tends them through drought.
One day her body does what bodies know —
she gasps at a heartbeat on a small gray screen.

And then the world tips, and she is standing
where you once stood: wrecked and full of wonder,
a child curved against her chest like a question,
still carrying the scent of somewhere else.

This is what we mean by circle.
Not that nothing changes — everything does.
But love keeps finding the same shape to pour itself into:
open arms, bent low, saying *here, I've got you, here.*
