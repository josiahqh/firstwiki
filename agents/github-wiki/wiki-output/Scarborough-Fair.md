## Overview

A traditional English ballad popularised by Simon & Garfunkel in 1966. Chosen as an entry point into modal harmony — specifically the Dorian mode — in a beautiful, accessible form.

## Musical Focus

**Primary concept:** [[Concept-Modal-Harmony|Modal Harmony]]

The song is in D Dorian — which sounds like D minor but with a raised 6th (B natural instead of B♭). That one note creates its characteristic bittersweet flavour.

**What to listen for:**
- The B natural in the melody
- The Em chord, which appears naturally in Dorian
- The open, medieval-sounding quality modal harmony gives

**Further listening:** Greensleeves (Dorian); Oye Como Va — Santana; So What — Miles Davis
