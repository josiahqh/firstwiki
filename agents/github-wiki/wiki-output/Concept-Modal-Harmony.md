# Modal Harmony

**Category:** Harmony · **Difficulty:** Intermediate

> Scales built on different degrees of the major scale — each with a distinct emotional colour.

---

## What It Is

Western music has seven modes. Each is simply the major scale started from a different note:

| Mode | Formula (vs major) | Character | Famous example |
|------|--------------------|-----------|----------------|
| **Ionian** | 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 | Bright, resolved — this *is* the major scale | Happy Birthday |
| **Dorian** | 1 2 ♭3 4 5 6 ♭7 | Minor but with a raised 6 — bittersweet, soulful | Scarborough Fair, So What |
| **Phrygian** | 1 ♭2 ♭3 4 5 ♭6 ♭7 | Dark, Spanish, tense | Flamenco |
| **Lydian** | 1 2 3 ♯4 5 6 7 | Dreamy, floating | Film scores |
| **Mixolydian** | 1 2 3 4 5 6 ♭7 | Major but unresolved — the rock mode | Norwegian Wood |
| **Aeolian** | 1 2 ♭3 4 5 ♭6 ♭7 | Dark, serious — natural minor scale | Stairway to Heaven |
| **Locrian** | 1 ♭2 ♭3 4 ♭5 ♭6 ♭7 | Dissonant, unstable | Rarely used |

## Dorian in Focus

The raised 6th gives Dorian warmth without brightness. To hear it: play a D minor scale, then make that B natural instead of B♭. That's D Dorian. Subtle, but distinct.
