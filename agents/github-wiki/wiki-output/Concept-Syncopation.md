# Syncopation

**Category:** Rhythm · **Difficulty:** Beginner–Intermediate

> Notes landing *between* the beats rather than on them — the engine of funk and groove.

---

## What It Is

In most Western music, the natural "strong" beats are 1 and 3 (in 4/4). Syncopation is the deliberate placement of accents or notes on the weak beats (2 and 4) or on the off-beats (the "ands") between beats. The listener's body still feels the underlying pulse — the syncopated notes create forward momentum and groove by arriving slightly *ahead* of or *behind* where the ear expects them.

## Why It Matters to Us

Syncopation is the defining characteristic of funk. Understanding it is the difference between *playing the notes* and *playing the groove*. When something feels stiff, syncopation is often what's missing.

## How to Practise It

1. Clap or sing the beat (1-2-3-4) while tapping the "ands" with your foot
2. Subdivide in your head: "1-and-2-and-3-and-4-and" — syncopated notes fall on the "ands"
3. Listen and air-play: pick a Stevie Wonder track and clap along to only the syncopated hits
4. Play it slowly — at 60% speed, syncopation loses its disguise
