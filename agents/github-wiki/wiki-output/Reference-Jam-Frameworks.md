# Jam Frameworks

Go-to structures for free improvisation — when you want to play without a plan.

---

## Framework 1 — Single Chord Drone

**Best for:** Opening up, exploring modes.

- Pick one root note (e.g. **A**)
- Bass or keys holds a static chord (Am7 or A5 for modal ambiguity)
- Everyone improvises freely — no chord changes
- Duration: 3–5 minutes

**Concept link:** [[Concept-Modal-Harmony|Modal Harmony]]

## Framework 2 — 12-Bar Blues in A

**Best for:** Improvisation practice, call-and-response.

```
| A7 | A7 | A7 | A7 |
| D7 | D7 | A7 | A7 |
| E7 | D7 | A7 | E7 |
```

- One person solos for a full 12 bars, then passes to the next

**Concept links:** [[Concept-Improvisation-Over-Changes|Improvisation Over Changes]] · [[Concept-Call-And-Response|Call and Response]]

## Framework 3 — Groove Vamp

**Best for:** Locking in rhythm section, groove work.

- 2-chord vamp (e.g. **Am7 → D7**)
- Bass and drums establish the groove for 4 bars — everyone listens
- No solos — just feel, balance, and space
- 5 minutes minimum

**Concept link:** [[Concept-Groove-Pocket|Groove & Pocket]]

## Framework 4 — Riff Pass

**Best for:** Ear training, creativity, fun.

- One person plays a 2-bar riff
- Next person copies it, then evolves it slightly
- Passes around the circle twice — no wrong answers

## Go-To Keys by Comfort

| Key | Good for |
|-----|----------|
| A minor / A major | Guitar (open position), keys |
| E minor | Guitar (open), raw rock feel |
| G major | Guitar (open), vocal range |
| D minor | Modal, folk, melancholy |
| C major | Keys (all white notes), beginners |
